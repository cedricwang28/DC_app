1800s

Used by the Missisaugas

The Oshawa Harbour was originally a marsh. It was first used by the Missisaugas in the early 18th Century

The Mississauga would load their furs onto their canoes at the mouth of the Oshawa River and travel to trading posts which were located at the mouth of the Credit River.

There they would barter the furs with the French, and later the English traders for supplies

1840

Harbour Construction

The first efforts to construct the harbour and a pier were made in 1840.

They were constructed by the Sydenham Harbour Company.

The Harbour Masters at this time were George A. Mothersill, the first Harbour Master (1841-1848), Joseph Wood (1849), and Thomas (Elder) Henry (1849-1854).

Mr. Henry was also one of the founders and a President of the Sydenham Harbour Company.

1844 - 1850

Variety of Exports

The harbour has been used to bring in various exports that varies from period to period.

Early on, the Missisaugas used the port to trade furs with the English and the French.

Main exports were flour, pork, ashes, oatmeal, whiskey, and even lumber.

Close

Mid 1850s

Ship Construction

The east side of the harbour was used to construct ships.

Shipbuilding was being done by David Annis, Daniel Conant and James and Abram Farewell.

They have built and launched several ships, namely the “Allies”, “Lord Durham”, “Caledonia” and “Paragon.”

1850s

Captains from Oshawa

Some of the residents who lived in Oshawa were also Captains, who sailed out of the harbour.

The captains include: Joseph Moore, Daniel Conant, and James and Abram Farewell
